# UI/UX Design 🎨

> Deciding how apps and websites *look* and *feel* — making technology beautiful,
> friendly, and easy for real people to use.

[← Back to all career paths](README.md)

---

## What is it?

Have you ever used an app that was **confusing and frustrating**? Or one that felt so smooth
you didn't even have to think? That difference is **UI/UX design**.

- **UI (User Interface)** — how it *looks*: colours, buttons, fonts, spacing, layout.
- **UX (User Experience)** — how it *feels* to use: is it easy? clear? does it make sense?

> 🎨 A designer's job is to make sure technology works for **humans** — not the other way around.

---

## What would I actually do?

- Talk to people to understand what they actually need
- Sketch screens on paper, then design them properly on a computer
- Choose colours, fonts, and layouts that feel right
- Build clickable **prototypes** to test ideas *before* any code is written
- Watch people use your design and improve the confusing parts

---

## Is this me? ✅

You might love UI/UX design if you:

- Notice when things are ugly, confusing, or badly laid out
- Care about **people's feelings** and how they experience things
- Enjoy art, drawing, or making things look good
- Like solving problems creatively **and** logically

> 💡 You don't have to be able to code to be a great designer. Empathy and creativity come first.

---

## Skills to build (in order)

1. **Design thinking** — start with the *user's* problem, not your solution
2. **Design basics** — colour, spacing, fonts (typography), and visual hierarchy
3. **A design tool** — **Figma** is free and the industry standard
4. **Wireframing & prototyping** — sketching screens and linking them together
5. **User testing** — watching real people use your design
6. **A little HTML/CSS** (optional) — helps you work with developers

## Starter projects 🚀

All of these can be done in **Figma** for free.

1. **Redesign a school form** — take a boring paper form and make a clean digital version.
2. **Design a phone app for your school** — home screen, timetable, and news.
3. **Make a clickable prototype** — link 3 screens so buttons "work" when clicked.
4. **App makeover** — pick an app you find ugly and redesign one screen to be better.
5. **Design a poster or event page** — practise layout, colour, and fonts.
