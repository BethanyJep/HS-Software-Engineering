# Session 2 – Career Conversations

**Term:** 2  
**Duration:** 90 minutes  
**Audience:** Grade 10 students, Alliance Girls High School  
**Facilitators:** TOFA / Alumni volunteers + 2–3 guest speakers  
**Format:** Panel discussion, Q&A, and small group conversations

---

## Session Objectives

By the end of this session, students will be able to:

1. Name and describe at least two different careers within software engineering and tech.
2. Understand the varied educational pathways into tech (university, bootcamp, self-taught, etc.).
3. Identify one person whose tech career story resonates with them.
4. Feel that a career in software engineering is achievable for someone like them.

---

## Materials Needed

- [ ] Projector / display screen
- [ ] Microphone (if large room)
- [ ] Speaker briefing notes (shared with speakers at least one week before)

- [ ] Sticky note question wall (students post Qs anonymously before session)
- [ ] Timer visible to the facilitator
- [ ] Optional: printed speaker bios for students

## Before the Session – Speaker Preparation

### Recruiting Speakers
Aim for 2–3 speakers from diverse backgrounds. Ideal mix:
- At least one **Alliance Girls alumna** working in tech (highest relatability)
- At least one person in a **non-coding tech role** (e.g., product manager, UX designer, data analyst)
- At least one person who took a **non-traditional path** (bootcamp, self-taught, career pivot)

Speakers can join **in person or via video call**.

### Speaker Briefing (share this with each speaker)

> **What we need from you:**
> - A brief intro (2–3 min): who you are and what you do
> - Your story (5–7 min): how you got into tech, including challenges you faced
> - One message for the students (1 min): what you wish you'd known at their age
>
> **Please prepare to answer questions like:**
> - "Did you always want to work in tech?"
> - "What does a typical day look like for you?"
> - "What subjects at school are most useful for your job?"
> - "What's the hardest part of your job?"
> - "What advice would you give to a Grade 10 student today?"
>
> **Tone:** Honest, warm, and encouraging. Share real challenges — students find authenticity more inspiring than perfection.

## Session Plan

### ⏱ 0–10 min | Welcome & Icebreaker

**Facilitator action:** Welcome students and introduce the session theme.

> 💡 See [Icebreakers](../resources/icebreakers.md). **Recommended:** *Dream Job Bingo* or *Would You Rather (Tech Edition)*.

Transition line:
> *"Last session we talked about what software engineering IS. Today we're going to meet real people doing it — and hear their honest stories."*

---

### ⏱ 10–15 min | Setting the Stage

Briefly recap Session 1 (2 min) and introduce the speakers (1 min each).

Before handing over to speakers, give students a focusing task:
> *"While you listen, use your Career Map worksheet to jot down: What does this person do? How did they get there? What stands out to you?"*

---

### ⏱ 15–50 min | Speaker Presentations & Panel

**Format:** Each speaker does a 5–8 minute story, then open panel Q&A.

**Suggested running order:**

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 15–23 min | Speaker 1 story (8 min) |
| 23–31 min | Speaker 2 story (8 min) |
| 31–37 min | Speaker 3 story (6 min) — if applicable |
| 37–50 min | Panel Q&A — students ask questions |

**Facilitating the Q&A:**
- Start with questions collected on the sticky note wall (anonymous = more honest questions).
- Then open the floor: *"Who has a question they'd like to ask directly?"*
- If silence, use prompts:
  - *"Could you tell us about a time things didn't go as planned?"*
  - *"Did you ever feel like you didn't belong in the tech world?"*
  - *"What's one thing students here can do THIS WEEK to start their journey?"*

---

### ⏱ 50–65 min | Small Group Conversations

**Purpose:** Deeper, more personal conversations that students often won't have in a big group.

**Instructions:**
1. Split students into small groups of 5–6.
2. Each group gets one speaker (rotate if time allows, or assign by interest).
3. Students can ask anything — no wrong questions.
4. Facilitators circulate to support and keep groups on track.

**Prompts on the table for each group:**
- "What surprised you about this person's journey?"
- "What skills do they use that we're already learning in school?"
- "If you could shadow them for one day, what would you want to see?"

---

### ⏱ 65–75 min | Career Map Share-Out

**Format:** Pair or trio share

Students share one thing from their Career Map:
> *"Share one thing you wrote down that surprised you, or one career path you'd want to learn more about."*

**Whole-room close:** Facilitator calls on 3–4 students to share briefly. Synthesise themes on the board.

---

### ⏱ 75–85 min | Pathways Into Tech

**Facilitator mini-talk (10 min):** Demystify the routes into a tech career.

Key pathways to cover:

| Pathway | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| University degree | Computer Science, Engineering, Information Technology | Many jobs, strong foundation |
| Coding bootcamp | Intensive short programme (3–6 months) | Great for career switchers |
| Online self-learning | FreeCodeCamp, Coursera, YouTube, Khan Academy | Free; requires discipline |
| Internships / attachments | Learn on the job while studying | Builds real-world experience |
| Starting a project | Build something — your portfolio speaks for itself | Open source, hackathons |

> *"The key message: there is NO single path. The best path is the one you actually take."*

Recommended resources to share:
- [freeCodeCamp.org](https://www.freecodecamp.org) – Free coding curriculum
- [CS50 (Harvard, free)](https://cs50.harvard.edu) – Best free intro to computer science
- [Andela Learning Community](https://www.andela.com) – Africa-focused tech training
- [Google Career Certificates](https://grow.google/certificates/) – Professional certificates
- [Coding for Everyone – KDC](https://kdc.go.ke) – Kenya-based digital training

---

### ⏱ 85–90 min | Wrap-Up & Preview of Session 3

> *"In our next and final foundation session, we're going to focus entirely on YOU — we'll set you up with the tools and resources you need to start learning on your own, and we'll form your Term 3 project groups."*

Encourage students to:
- Follow or connect with the speakers (if speakers share contact info or LinkedIn)
- Explore one of the resources shared this week
- Come to Session 3 with a problem idea in mind

## Facilitator Notes

- Brief speakers thoroughly — they should know the age group and the goals of the session.
- Encourage speakers to be honest about failure and doubt; perfection is not inspiring, persistence is.
- If a speaker drops out last minute, use a recorded talk (e.g., a TED talk by an African woman in tech).
  - Suggested: *"How I Became a Software Engineer"* talks on YouTube by African creators
  - Suggested: Lola Prewitt-White, Wanjiru Kamau-Rutenberg, or local Kenyan tech leaders
- Have a backup speaker from the facilitation team who can share their own story.
- Manage time firmly during the panel — it's easy to run over.
- Collect Career Map worksheets — they're useful input for coaching students in Term 3.
